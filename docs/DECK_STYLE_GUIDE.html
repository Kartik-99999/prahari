The one rule
Light paper, a calm serif headline, one bold accent per slide, and a lot of air. Numbers get the hero treatment. Nothing is cramped; every slide makes one argument.

Palette

Doing it in PowerPoint / Keynote / Slides

The look is achievable with native shapes — no plugins.

Saffron band

A full-width rectangle → Format Shape → Gradient fill, direction top-to-bottom, 4 stops: #F8F8FA 0% → #E87040 30% → #EC A862 70% → #F8F8FA 100%. The transparent ends make it float.

Gold numbers

Type the number in the serif, then Text Fill → Gradient: #8A6A3E → #C8A878 → #9C7C4E at ~160°. On the saffron band, use solid #FFFDF9 instead.

Pills & the nav

Rounded Rectangle → drag the corner handle to full radius. Nav = a white pill across the top with a soft shadow; primary button fill #111827, ghost = white with a #D8DBE2 1.5 pt outline.

Soft cards & shadows

Rounded rectangle, corner radius ≈ 0.25″, fill white, no line. Shadow: outer, blur ~30, distance ~12, transparency ~86%, colour navy. Subtle — you should barely see it.

Do & don’t

Do

  • Keep slides light & airy — one argument each.
  • Let the serif headline + one big number carry the slide.
  • Use the real console screenshots (already in the repo) inside soft white cards.
  • Reserve green for “contained/prevented,” red for “threat.”
  • Lead with the honest evidence order: external benchmark → frozen transfer → in-domain.

Don’t

  • No dark “hacker” slides, neon, or matrix rain — this brand is calm daylight.
  • Don’t bold the serif or stretch it — weight 400, real letterforms.
  • Don’t use green/red/indigo as decoration — they mean things.
  • No hard borders or drop-shadowed clip-art; soft cards only.
  • Don’t crowd — if a slide has two ideas, it’s two slides.
